A PERSONAL MISSION

Punching Up Real Life

What We Do

Plot Swap is a solo experiment in human connection. I design, write, and direct scripted theater disguised as real life – not to trick you, but to surprise you with a story when you least expect it.

In a world dominated by screens, I believe people deserve moments that feel real and undeniable. Whether it's a quiet confession in a bar or an elaborate mystery in a barbershop, these performances are my gift to Houston. I want to turn your ordinary Tuesday into a memory you'll keep forever.

We started with interactive theater (Pheromones, 2025), learned what resonated, and evolved into hidden performances that feel like real life.

"Analog entertainment deserves another chance."
RUSSELL VARNER // HOUSTON

Meet the Architect

I'm a Universal Designer. Most days, I'm building video games or solving complex design problems. But I've always felt that the most important "interface" we have is each other.

As a kid, my imagination often got me into trouble. Today, I use that same restless energy to unlock the potential in the ordinary. I don't have a big production company or a marketing team. It’s just me, a few dedicated actors, and a belief that this type of entertainment deserves another chance.

If you've ever looked at a local business or a city street and asked, "What if this could be more?" – then you and I speak the same language.

My slogan: Punch Up Real Life.

Why Word of Mouth Matters

We're transparent about what we do. But how you discover us is part of the experience. The best stories spread through friends, curiosity, and chance encounters, not algorithms.

For Local Spaces

If you own a bar, a shop, or a space that feels like it has a story to tell, I’d love to help you tell it. I partner with local businesses to create immersive experiences that bring people together in meaningful, face-to-face ways. Let’s make something unforgettable.

Sustain the Secret

Every Plot Swap performance is an investment in our community’s story. Because these shows are free to discover and hide in plain sight, they rely entirely on the generosity of people who believe in the mission.

If you want to help me keep "punching up real life" in Houston, consider supporting the journey. Every dollar goes directly into production costs, actor stipends, and keeping the theater alive on our streets.
