The Mission.

What We Do.

We perform scripted theater disguised as real life. You might witness a breakup at a coffee shop, a mysterious conversation at a barbershop, or a stranger's confession at a bar. It's all scripted, but it feels real.

People deserve stories. Not just to watch, but to be part of. When theater hides in plain sight, it becomes a memory you carry with you—something that happened to you, not something you paid to see.

We started with interactive theater (Pheromones, 2025), learned what resonated, and evolved into hidden performances that feel like real life.

"Analog entertainment deserves another chance."

Meet Russell Varner

I'm a Universal Designer who sees potential where others see the ordinary. As a kid, my storytelling got me in trouble. As an adult, it became my superpower.

I can't help but reimagine everything. Turning a farmer's market stall into theater, a wedding into an experience, a simple party into a memory people talk about for years. Whether I'm designing countless video games, crafting immersive performances, or building social experiments that blur the line between art and life, I'm always asking the same question: What if this could be more?

My slogan: Punch Up Real Life.

Why Word of Mouth Matters

We're transparent about what we do. But how you discover us is part of the experience. The best stories spread through friends, curiosity, and chance encounters—not algorithms.

You'll find us the old-fashioned way: through a friend who saw something strange, a flyer in an unexpected place, or sheer luck. Because analog entertainment deserves another chance.
