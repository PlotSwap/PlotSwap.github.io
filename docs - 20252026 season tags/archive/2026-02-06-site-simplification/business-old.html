Quick Questions

We'll align on boundaries and a simple run-of-show before performance night.

Will this disrupt service?

We design the scene around your normal flow. Most pilot nights are low-footprint: one table or small zone, no blocking walkways, no shouting, no "stop the room" moments. Guests can keep eating and drinking. If you want bigger energy, we can scale it up—but the default is minimal disruption.

Do staff need to be in on it?

A manager and 1–2 key staff should know what's happening so operations stay smooth. Everyone else can stay genuine. The performance is fully scripted and pre-planned, so we can brief staff on what to expect, where to steer guests, and what not to do.

What's the tone?

The tone is written for your venue and your crowd. We can do grounded and believable, comedic, romantic, or tense, but it will always be calibrated to your brand. We'll confirm boundaries in advance (language, sexuality, volume, themes). If your venue is family-friendly, we keep it PG. If it's 21+, we can push further—only with your approval.

What about filming + guest consent?

Filming is optional. If you approve it, we keep it lightweight and respectful:

  • We use clear signage and/or a designated "filming zone" so guests aren't surprised.
  • We avoid close-ups of anyone who isn't comfortable.
  • You approve any footage before it's posted or used publicly.

If you'd rather not film at all, we can still run the pilot.

What do you need from us on the day-of?

Very little. Typically:

  • A small zone/table we agree on in advance
  • A manager point person we can reach
  • 10–15 minutes before start for a quick staff briefing
  • Permission to operate in your normal environment (no special tech required)

We bring everything else. No stage, no lighting, no sound system unless you want it.
